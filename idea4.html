Intelligent Waste Classification & Recycling System

The Intelligent Waste Classification & Recycling System aims to improve waste management in smart cities by automatically classifying waste, optimizing collection routes, and increasing recycling efficiency.

Design and Analysis of Algorithms (DAA) is applied to manage large-scale waste data, reduce transportation cost, ensure timely collection, and prioritize hazardous waste processing.

Graph – City Waste Collection Network

Graphs represent waste collection zones as nodes and roads as edges, enabling efficient modeling of the city waste infrastructure.

Breadth First Search (BFS) – Area Coverage

BFS ensures systematic and level-wise coverage of all waste bins, preventing missed collection points.

Depth First Search (DFS) – Deep Zone Inspection

DFS helps in deep inspection of specific zones to detect illegal dumping and overflow conditions.

Kruskal’s Algorithm – Minimum Cost Collection Network

Kruskal’s algorithm constructs a minimum spanning tree that minimizes fuel and transportation cost during waste collection.
