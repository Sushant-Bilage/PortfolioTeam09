Intelligent Water Flow & Leak Management System

This system manages urban water pipelines to minimize leakage, reduce installation cost, and ensure efficient water distribution in a smart city.

Design and Analysis of Algorithms (DAA) is used to model pipeline networks, optimize routing cost, detect leaks, and prioritize emergency response.

Graph – Water Network Modeling

Junctions are nodes and pipelines are weighted edges.

Kruskal’s Algorithm

Finds the minimum cost pipeline layout.

Dijkstra’s Algorithm

Computes the shortest water supply path from reservoirs.

Bellman-Ford Algorithm

Detects leaks using negative cycle detection.

Floyd–Warshall Algorithm

Optimizes water flow between all junction pairs.

Priority Queue

Handles emergency zones based on severity.

Hash Table

Maps sensor IDs to zones with constant-time access.

Queue

Processes leak alerts in FIFO order.

Introsort (std::sort)

Ranks zones by pressure drop and water usage.
