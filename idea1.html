Structured Sports Data Management System

The Structured Sports Data Management System is designed to manage player profiles, match schedules, rankings, and tournament progression in an efficient and organized manner.

The system addresses the problem of unorganized sports events by applying optimized data structures and algorithms for fast access, fair scheduling, and scalability.

It is developed using core Design and Analysis of Algorithms (DAA) concepts, making it suitable for real-world deployment.

Who Can Use This System?

• City Sports Authorities
• Sports Clubs & Academies
• Schools & Colleges
• Event Organizers
• Citizens & Amateur Players

Why Design & Analysis of Algorithms?

• Efficient handling of large datasets
• Reduced time complexity
• Optimized scheduling and rankings
• Scalable sports management

Hash Table

Used to store and retrieve player information efficiently.

Priority Queue

Used to rank players based on performance.

Linked List

Used to manage dynamic match schedules.

Graph, DFS & BFS

Used to represent and traverse tournament structures.

Dijkstra Algorithm

Used to find optimal or shortest paths for scheduling and logistics.

Sorting & Searching

Used to organize leaderboards and quickly retrieve sports statistics.
